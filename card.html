Macaw

Macaws live an average of 50 years, but can live up to 70 years if kept in captivity.

  • Average Lifespan: 50 years
  • Average Length: 3.3 ft.
  • Conservation Status: Least Concern
  • Habitat: Rainforest

Macaws are romantic and faithful. They are a monogamous species, this means that they only have one partner throughout their lives. The body language of these birds is very clear when they begin to form pairs: the duo separates from the others, they will begin to groom and feed each other and will have dilated pupils.
